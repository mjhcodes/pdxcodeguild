It was the Lebanese-born poet and painter, Kahlil Gibran, who once uttered, "all our words are but crumbs that fall down from the feast of the mind."

They are thoughts thrust into existence. Actions have claimed to speak louder, the pen mightier, but true power continues to lay with the word. In its capacity to inspire and motivate, to denigrate and condemn.

As part of my computer programming course at the PDX Code Guild, we were recently tasked with constructing a project to signal the shift of focus from the back-end to the front-end; from the approachable language of Python to the upcoming structure and artistry of HTML & CSS.

We Start With Zero

Sunday, July 7th, 2019

It's difficult to know where to begin. After all, I, myself, am a beginner...

Tomorrow, I will walk through the doors of the PDX Code Guild to attend the 1st day of the rest of my life the school's flagship Full Stack Developer Bootcamp; three months of full-time, intensive education. And then, the work truly begins, as I further inch toward my goal of transitioning from the world of hotels and customer service to that of computer programming.

Somewhere along the way, I happened to pick up a tip for those new to coding. It involved "The Protégé Effect," the idea that the best way to learn a given subject is to teach it. From my experience coaching American football while rooted in Manchester, England, I can personally vouch for the accuracy of this claim. The depth of detail to which I was required to dive in order to simplify the sport to its most fundamental level was illuminating. It required flexibility, commitment, and, above all, patience. And, thus, in that spirit, this blog was born.

ABOUT

Born and raised in Sonoma County, California, Matthew is a veteran of the U.S. Air Force and holds dual citizenship with the United Kingdom.

Upon graduating from California State University, Chico with a Bachelor's of Science degree in Recreation Administration, he spent the following ten years exploring the world of travel, tourism and hospitality; touching forty-one countries in the process.

In the summer of 2019, Matthew moved to Portland, Oregon to attend the Full Stack Developer Bootcamp at the PDX Code Guild, from which he will graduate in mid-October.
